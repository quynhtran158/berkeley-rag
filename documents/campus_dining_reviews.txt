POST: "Honest dining hall review after 4 semesters" (r/berkeley, 2024)

I've eaten at every dining hall on campus. Here's the truth:

CROSSROADS (Unit 3, Southside):
Best overall dining hall on campus by a significant margin. Has the best variety and the longest hours. The grill station (burgers, fries) is solid. The international station rotates dishes — Vietnamese pho days are the best days. Wait times: 10-15 minutes during lunch (11:30am-1:30pm), 5-10 minutes outside of peak hours. Downside: gets extremely crowded during finals week. The seating area fills up fast. Pro tip: go at 5pm when the dinner rush hasn't started. The salad bar is fresh and extensive.

CLARK KERR (Clark Kerr campus, 10 min walk from main campus):
Underrated and chronically underused by people who don't live near it. Less crowded than Crossroads. The pasta station is consistently good. Stir-fry made to order on Thursdays. Quiet enough to study while eating. The dessert rotation is better than other halls — actual pies and tarts, not just soft-serve. Distance is the only downside.

FOOTHILL (Northside):
The "forgotten" dining hall. Beautiful outdoor seating with a view of the hills. Food quality is similar to Crossroads but with less variety. Worth going to on nice days. Almost never crowded. The breakfast is the best on campus — fresh omelets made to order until 10am. If you live Northside, this should be your primary dining hall.

CAFE 3 (Unit 3, Southside):
Worst of the four main halls. Limited options, mediocre quality, smallest space. People only go here when Crossroads is too crowded. The pizza station operates only 5-9pm. The one advantage: it's right next to the Unit 3 dorms, so zero travel time if you live there.

Yelp Review (Crossroads Dining, 4/5 stars):
"Finally, a college dining hall that doesn't make me hate food. The waffle station at breakfast is genuinely good — fresh waffles with proper toppings. The variety has improved since they added the international station. Wait times can be brutal during the lunch rush (noon-1pm specifically). The late-night hours (until 11pm on weekdays) are a lifesaver. Meal swipes work here and the system is easy — tap your card and go."

Yelp Review (Foothill Dining, 4/5 stars):
"The best-kept secret on campus. Never crowded, always clean, beautiful setting. Breakfast until 10am is the move. The omelette station is real — you tell them what to put in, they make it in front of you. Not a lot of variety for lunch but what they have is fresh. The walk from central campus takes about 12 minutes but it's worth it for a peaceful meal."

DINING HALL HOURS (as of Fall 2024):
- Crossroads: M-F 7am-10pm, Sat-Sun 9am-8pm
- Clark Kerr: M-F 7am-9pm, Sat-Sun 9am-7pm  
- Foothill: M-F 7am-9pm, Sat-Sun 9am-7pm
- Cafe 3: M-F 5pm-11pm (dinner only, closed weekends)

TIPS:
- Avoid Crossroads 12pm-1pm. Worst wait times of the week.
- All halls accept meal plan swipes AND Dining Dollars (flexible currency)
- Guest swipes exist but are limited — confirm with your hall
- Most halls have "make-it" stations where you request specific prep
